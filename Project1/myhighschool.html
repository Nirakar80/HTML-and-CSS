Budhanilkantha School, often referred to as BNKS, is a public boarding school in Nepal. It is located in Narayansthan, Kathmandu on the foothills of Shivapuri mountain (2,732 metres). It is named after the Budhanilkantha Temple which is located nearby. One-third of students are granted scholarships based on merit and need, after an entrance examination held in all the 75 districts of Nepal. Located at the foot of Shivapuri reservation, it is one of the epoitome of excellence in Nepal. It was established by the joint efforts of the british and nepali government with the hopes to provied the country with effective and effiencent leaders. The first batch of students took the School Leaving Certificate Examination in 1980. In 1983, English was made the official language of communicationa and instruction to expose the students to the vast international community. The University of Cambridge(CIE) O-Level and A-Level programs were introduced in 1985. The school is a full boarding school for students from 5th grade to A-Level, aged 10 to 19. Although the school is governed as a Public Educational Trust, it charges tuition fees for most students and also receives funding from the Ministry of Education. This makes the school a blend of private and public institution.
